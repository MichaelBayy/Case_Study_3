To make the classes more clear, the 0 and 1 were changes to Flop and Hit respectively. The amount of each class was then checked.

#change popularity to Hit or Flop
song_table$popularity_f = as.character(song_table$popularity)
song_table$popularity_f[song_table$popularity_f == 0] = "Flop"
song_table$popularity_f[song_table$popularity_f == 1] = "Hit"

#split of Hit or Flop in dataset
pop_count = song_table %>% group_by(popularity_f) %>% dplyr::summarize(ncount = n())

There is a strong inverse correlation between acousticness and energy and loudness. For the classification model only acousticness will be kept.

The other strong correlation is between sections and duration_s. Only sections will be kept for the model.
